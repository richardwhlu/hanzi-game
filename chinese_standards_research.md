# International Chinese Language Learning Standards & Resources Research

## Research Summary

This document compiles information about major international language learning standards bodies and their Chinese language resources, including downloadable materials, character lists, and proficiency frameworks.

---

## 1. International Baccalaureate (IB) Chinese Language Standards

### Overview
The IB offers Chinese as part of their Language Acquisition programme in the Diploma Programme.

### Key Resources
- **IB Chinese Language and Literature Guide**: Available for registered schools
- **Assessment Criteria**: Uses criterion-referenced assessment
- **Proficiency Levels**: Aligned with IB Language Acquisition framework

### Downloadable Resources
**Primary Source**: [IB Programme Resource Centre](https://resources.ibo.org)
- **File Format**: PDF documents (password protected for registered schools)
- **Character Lists**: Not publicly available - integrated into curriculum guides
- **Licensing**: Restricted to IB World Schools
- **Access**: Requires school registration and login

### Character/Vocabulary Framework
- **Beginner Level**: ~800-1000 characters
- **Intermediate Level**: ~1500-2000 characters  
- **Advanced Level**: ~2500+ characters
- **Assessment**: Focus on communicative competence rather than character count

## 2. ACTFL (American Council on Teaching Foreign Languages)

### Overview
ACTFL provides proficiency guidelines and assessment tools for Chinese language learning.

### Key Standards Documents
- **ACTFL Proficiency Guidelines 2012 - Chinese**: Defines proficiency levels
- **Chinese Can-Do Statements**: Performance indicators by proficiency level

### Proficiency Levels
- **Novice**: Low, Mid, High
- **Intermediate**: Low, Mid, High  
- **Advanced**: Low, Mid, High
- **Superior**
- **Distinguished**

### Downloadable Resources

#### 1. ACTFL Proficiency Guidelines
- **URL**: [https://www.actfl.org/resources/actfl-proficiency-guidelines-2012](https://www.actfl.org/resources/actfl-proficiency-guidelines-2012)
- **File Format**: PDF
- **Content**: Detailed proficiency descriptors for Chinese
- **Licensing**: Available for educational use
- **Status**: ✅ Publicly accessible

#### 2. Chinese Can-Do Statements

- **Licensing**: Free for educational use
- **Status**: ✅ Publicly accessible

### Character/Vocabulary Estimates

- **Superior/Distinguished**: 3000+ characters

## 3. CEFR-Adapted Chinese Language Frameworks

### Overview
The Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) has been adapted for Chinese language learning by various European institutions.

### Key Frameworks

#### European Centre for Modern Languages (ECML)
- **Project**: "Chinese as a Foreign Language in European Schools"
- **URL**: [https://www.ecml.at/Resources/ECMLresources/tabid/277/language/en-GB/Default.aspx](https://www.ecml.at/Resources/ECMLresources/tabid/277/language/en-GB/Default.aspx)

#### CEFR Levels for Chinese
- **A1 (Breakthrough)**: ~200-300 characters
- **A2 (Waystage)**: ~400-600 characters
- **B1 (Threshold)**: ~800-1200 characters
- **B2 (Vantage)**: ~1500-2000 characters
- **C1 (Proficiency)**: ~2500-3500 characters
- **C2 (Mastery)**: ~4000+ characters

### Downloadable Resources
- **CEFR Descriptors**: Available in multiple languages
- **Chinese-specific adaptations**: Limited public availability
- **File Format**: PDF documents
- **Licensing**: Generally open for educational use

## 4. HSK (Hanyu Shuiping Kaoshi) - Chinese Proficiency Test

### Overview
The official Chinese government standardized test for Chinese language proficiency for non-native speakers.

### Current HSK System (2021 Revision)
- **Levels**: HSK 1-9 (previously 1-6)
- **Character Requirements**: Clearly defined for each level

### Official Resources

#### HSK Vocabulary Lists
- **Official Source**: [http://www.chinesetest.cn/](http://www.chinesetest.cn/)
- **International Site**: [https://www.hskk.org/](https://www.hskk.org/)

### Character/Word Requirements by Level
- **HSK 1**: 150 words, ~100 characters
- **HSK 2**: 300 words, ~200 characters
- **HSK 3**: 600 words, ~400 characters
- **HSK 4**: 1200 words, ~800 characters
- **HSK 5**: 2500 words, ~1500 characters
- **HSK 6**: 5000 words, ~2500 characters
- **HSK 7-9**: Progressive expansion to ~4000+ characters

### Downloadable Resources

#### Official HSK Vocabulary Lists
- **URL**: Available through official HSK websites
- **File Format**: PDF, Excel spreadsheets
- **Content**: Word lists with pinyin and English translations
- **Licensing**: Official materials, free for test preparation
- **Quality**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Highest quality, official standard)
