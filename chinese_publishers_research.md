# Chinese Language Textbook Publishers Research

## Overview
Research on character lists and progression models from major Chinese language textbook publishers for educational game development.

---

## 1. People's Education Press (人民教育出版社, PEP)

### Company Background
- Founded: 1950
- Primary role: China's leading educational publisher for K-12 textbooks
- Official website: http://www.pep.com.cn/
- Status: State-owned publisher under Ministry of Education

### Primary Chinese Textbook Series

#### 统编语文 (Tongbian Yuwen) - Unified Chinese Language Series
- **Publisher**: People's Education Press
- **Target**: Grades 1-9 (Primary and Junior Secondary)
- **Status**: Current national standard textbooks (implemented since 2017-2019)
- **Official Name**: 义务教育教科书·语文 (Compulsory Education Textbooks - Chinese)

#### Character Progression Model
**Grade 1 (Age 6-7)**:
- Semester 1: ~300 characters (recognition), ~100 characters (writing)
- Semester 2: ~400 characters (recognition), ~200 characters (writing)
- Focus: Basic stroke orders, simple pictographs, high-frequency characters

**Grade 2 (Age 7-8)**:
- Total: ~800 characters (recognition), ~300 characters (writing)
- Introduction of compound characters and radicals

**Grade 3 (Age 8-9)**:
- Total: ~1200 characters (recognition), ~400 characters (writing)
- More complex characters, introduction to dictionary skills

**Grades 4-6 (Ages 9-12)**:
- Progressive increase to ~2500 characters (recognition)
- ~1800 characters (writing)
- Focus on character families and etymological connections

#### Available Resources
- **Digital Resources**: http://bp.pep.com.cn/ (Basic Education Platform)
- **Teacher Manuals**: Available with detailed character lists and progression charts
- **Character Lists**: Included in curriculum standards documents
- **Sample Materials**: Some units available on official website

## 2. Beijing Language University Press (北京语言大学出版社, BLUP)

### Company Background
- Founded: 1985
- Specialization: Chinese as Foreign Language (CFL) materials
- University affiliation: Beijing Language and Culture University
- Website: http://www.blcup.com/

### Primary Chinese Language Series

#### For Native Speakers:
**小学语文 (Primary School Chinese)**
- Similar progression to national standards
- Character counts aligned with Ministry of Education guidelines

#### For Foreign Learners:

**New Practical Chinese Reader (新实用汉语课本)**
- **Levels**: Elementary, Intermediate, Advanced (6 volumes total)
- **Characters**: Progressive from ~500 to 2000+ characters
- **Approach**: Communicative methodology with character component analysis

**HSK Standard Course (HSK标准教程)**
- **Levels**: HSK 1-6 aligned
- **Character Counts**:
  - HSK 1: 174 characters
  - HSK 2: 347 characters (cumulative)
  - HSK 3: 617 characters (cumulative)
  - HSK 4: 1064 characters (cumulative)
  - HSK 5: 1685 characters (cumulative)
  - HSK 6: 2500+ characters (cumulative)

**Developing Chinese (发展汉语)**
- **Levels**: Elementary, Intermediate, Advanced
- **Focus**: Integrated skills development
- **Character Introduction**: Thematic and functional approach

#### Character Progression Model
- **Frequency-based**: Most common characters introduced first
- **Radical-based**: Characters grouped by shared components
- **Thematic**: Characters organized around topics and situations
- **Stroke-based**: Simple characters before complex ones

#### Available Resources
- **Digital Platform**: BLCU Online courses and apps
- **Downloadable Materials**: Some character lists available
- **Teacher Resources**: Comprehensive teaching guides
- **Assessment Tools**: Character recognition and writing practice materials

## 3. Commercial Press (商务印书馆)

### Company Background
- Founded: 1897
- Historic status: China's oldest modern publisher
- Focus: Dictionaries, reference works, and educational materials
- Website: http://www.cp.com.cn/

### Primary Chinese Language Materials

#### Reference Works:
**现代汉语词典 (Contemporary Chinese Dictionary)**
- Standard reference for character usage
- ~13,000 characters covered
- Authoritative source for character definitions and usage

**新华字典 (Xinhua Dictionary)**
- Most widely used character dictionary
- ~11,000+ characters
- Standard for character stroke orders and pronunciations

#### Educational Series:
**商务馆小学语文 (Commercial Press Primary Chinese)**
- Alternative textbook series
- Character progression similar to national standards
- Focus on classical literature integration

#### Character Resources

## Common Progression Patterns Across Publishers

### Grade-Level Standards (National Guidelines)
Based on Chinese Ministry of Education standards that most publishers follow:

| Grade Level | Recognition Characters | Writing Characters | Cumulative Total (Recognition) |
|-------------|----------------------|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| Grade 1     | 400                  | 100               | 400                           |
| Grade 2     | 800                  | 300               | 800                           |
| Grade 3     | 1200                 | 400               | 1200                          |
| Grade 4     | 1600                 | 800               | 1600                          |
| Grade 5     | 2000                 | 1200              | 2000                          |
| Grade 6     | 2500                 | 1600              | 2500                          |

### Character Introduction Principles
1. **Frequency First**: Most commonly used characters introduced early
2. **Stroke Progression**: Simple strokes before complex combinations
3. **Radical Families**: Characters sharing components taught together
4. **Semantic Fields**: Related meanings grouped thematically
5. **Phonetic Components**: Characters with shared pronunciations clustered

### Common Character Categories by Learning Stage

#### Stage 1 (Grades 1-2): Foundation Characters
- Numbers: 一二三四五六七八九十
- Basic actions: 看见听说走跑
- Family: 爸妈哥姐弟妹
- Body parts: 头手脚眼口
- Nature: 山水火土木

#### Stage 2 (Grades 3-4): Expansion Characters
- School subjects and tools
- More complex family relationships
- Abstract concepts (time, emotions)
- Compound characters with familiar radicals

#### Stage 3 (Grades 5-6): Advanced Characters
- Academic vocabulary
- Literary and cultural characters
- Characters with multiple meanings
- Classical Chinese elements

## Key Insights for Game Development

### Character Selection Priorities
1. **High Frequency**: Focus on most commonly used characters across all publishers
2. **Progressive Complexity**: Maintain stroke count and radical complexity progression
3. **Thematic Coherence**: Group characters by meaning or usage context
4. **Cultural Relevance**: Include characters essential for cultural understanding

### Learning Progression Models
- **Spiral Approach**: Reintroduce characters in increasing complexity
- **Component Building**: Teach radicals and components systematically
- **Contextual Learning**: Present characters within meaningful contexts
- **Multi-modal Input**: Combine visual, auditory, and kinesthetic learning

### Assessment Integration
- **Recognition vs. Production**: Different levels of character mastery
- **Contextual Usage**: Characters in phrases and sentences
- **Cultural Competence**: Characters within cultural contexts
- **Transfer Skills**: Application to new contexts and materials
